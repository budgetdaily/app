Getting Started

How BudgetDaily works

BudgetDaily is built around one idea: knowing how much you can spend today without going over budget by month's end. Here's what you need to know.

Overview

What to set up, and what to keep doing

Set up once

1

Add an account

Create a checking or savings account. This is where your transactions will be recorded and your balance tracked.

2

Add a recurring income transaction If applicable

If you receive a regular salary, add it as a recurring transaction for an accurate cash flow picture. Skip this if your income varies — freelance, casual, or part-time work.

3

Create a budget

Set a weekly, fortnightly, or monthly spending limit for a category — like Groceries or Eating Out. Budgets unlock the Daily Balance and pace tracking.

4

Add recurring expenses

Add subscriptions and regular bills as recurring transactions. These are factored into your daily rate so your available balance already accounts for committed spending.

Ongoing

Log your spending as it happens

Add a transaction each time you spend. BudgetDaily updates your daily balance in real time — the more consistently you log, the more accurate your picture.

The Daily Balance

The core number BudgetDaily is built around

What is the Daily Balance?

How much you can safely spend right now and stay on pace. It's your accrued budget so far (daily rate × days elapsed) plus any rollover, minus what you've already spent. Positive = ahead of pace. Negative = spent more than accrued so far.

Daily rate: your budget limit ÷ days in the period, minus recurring committed expenses. A $70 weekly budget = $10/day base rate.

Budgets

Track spending by category over a period

Each budget tracks one category over a weekly, fortnightly, or monthly period. Unspent budget rolls over — overspending is carried forward as a deduction.

The spending ring in budget detail shows how much of your period budget you've used. Green = under budget. Amber = within rollover savings. Red = exhausted.

Daily Limit = remaining budget ÷ days left including today. Higher than your base rate means you've been spending less than expected.

Accounts

Your financial picture in one place

The balance shows money in your account today — future-dated transactions are excluded so you see what you actually have right now.

A ~ projected balance appears below your current balance when future-dated transactions are scheduled to settle.

BudgetDaily supports loan and investment accounts — net worth is calculated automatically across all types.

Adding transactions

Log spending quickly

Long-press any category or account chip in the transaction form to instantly save — no extra taps. Fill in the amount first.

Use recurring transactions for subscriptions and fixed bills — they count against your daily rate automatically.

Tips & tricks

A few things worth knowing early

Enable iCloud Sync (Pro) to keep data across devices. Your data goes to your private Apple account — never our servers.

Use account tags to group accounts (e.g. Personal / Business) and filter your dashboard.

Link a budget to a savings goal — surplus at period end flows automatically to the goal.
